Who I Am

About Gage Sampson

Gage Sampson is an entrepreneur from Franklin Furnace, Ohio, co-founder of TikTok Titans — a TikTok Shop Partnered Agency — educator, and a leader in the creator economy and AI-driven marketing systems. Through TikTok Titans, Gage has helped generate over $5 million in gross merchandise value (GMV) for brands while coaching hundreds of creators to monetize their content on TikTok Shop.

Before entering entrepreneurship, Gage was a standout basketball player at Green Local School District in Franklin Furnace, earning All-Ohio honors — a testament to his competitive drive and discipline. That athletic foundation, built in a small town of about 1,500 people as one of seven siblings, shaped the work ethic he later channeled into business. After high school, Gage transitioned through barbering and early viral success on TikTok before recognizing the massive opportunity in TikTok Shop and e-commerce, ultimately co-founding TikTok Titans and building AI-powered marketing systems that help creators and brands scale.

Today, Gage Sampson works with brands like Dude Wipes, Goli, Dr. Biocare, Selerb, Nello Supercalm, and NeuroGum, while coaching creators through the Titans Inner Circle. Whether it's running high-performing TikTok Shop campaigns, leveraging AI to build new tools, or helping creators secure brand deals, his mission is simple: help creators win, help brands grow, and keep building the future of digital marketing.

The Journey

Gage Sampson's Story

01

Early Years

I grew up in Franklin Furnace, Ohio, a very small town with just around 1,500 people. As one of seven siblings, five brothers and one sister, I went to Green Local School District. Sports were everything. Basketball and football were not just hobbies, they were identity, and I earned All State honors in basketball. But even while chasing wins on the court, I always had one thing clear in my mind: I wanted to make money.

02

The Barber Days

After high school, I was pushed toward barbering because my dad was a barber and owned a barber shop in Willardburg, Ohio. Following in his footsteps, I went to barber school and started my first career cutting hair.

03

Viral Breakthrough

In 2020, I posted a random skit video on TikTok, and within 24 hours it exploded. It got 10 million views, which led to more viral skits, and by the end of the month I had grown my account to about 230,000 followers. TikTok was not monetizable then, so I returned to the barber chair. But the spark for digital growth never left.

04

Sales Mastery

By 2023, that spark turned into fire. I hit the road during Taylor Swift's Eras Tour, selling bracelets as a traveling vendor with my best friend. That experience taught me a huge lesson in sales: just because I would not buy something does not mean no one else will. There is a market out there for almost anything, and as long as you know how to sell it, you can make money.

05

TikTok Titans

Then in early 2024, I saw TikTok Shop emerging, knew it was the future of e-commerce, and a few months later co-founded TikTok Titans. Ever since, we have been scaling creators and brands to entirely new levels.
